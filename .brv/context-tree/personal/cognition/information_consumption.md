## Raw Concept
**Task:**
Define principles for information consumption and cognitive health

**Changes:**
- Established principle: Don't let algorithms define importance

**Flow:**
Algorithm push -> Evaluation of relevance -> Conscious filtering -> Focus preservation

**Timestamp:** 2026-03-07

## Narrative
### Structure
The human brain lacks the capacity for real-time global event processing. Algorithms exploit this by pushing irrelevant "urgent" content.

### Highlights
Truly important information repeats itself and doesn't require "breaking" alerts to be noticed.

### Rules
Rule 1: Do not let algorithms define what is important.
Rule 2: Ignore most "breaking" news (90% is irrelevant).

## Facts
- **cognitive_limitation**: Human brain is not designed for real-time processing of global breaking news [personal]
- **information_relevance**: 90% of 'breaking' or 'urgent' content pushed by algorithms is irrelevant to the individual [project]
- **information_consequences**: Constant exposure to breaking news leads to anxiety, decision fatigue, and decreased focus [personal]
