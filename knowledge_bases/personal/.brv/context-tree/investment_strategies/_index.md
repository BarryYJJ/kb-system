# Barbell Strategy: Structural Summary (Level D2)

The Barbell Strategy is a bimodal risk management framework (Nassim Taleb, *Antifragile*) characterized by the aggressive avoidance of "middle-ground" risk in favor of extreme positions.

### Core Allocation & Architecture
- **Conservative Pillar (90%):** Capital preservation via hyper-safe assets (bonds, cash). Defines the strategy's floor.
- **Aggressive Pillar (10%):** High-convexity, high-reward speculation (startups, derivatives). Captures unlimited upside.
- **Antifragility:** The relationship between these extremes creates a system that benefits from volatility while limiting downside.

### Critical Rules & Modern Shifts
- **The "Middle" Exclusion:** Strictly zero allocation to medium-risk positions, which are viewed as providing neither safety nor significant alpha.
- **AI Era Application:** Modern extremes focus on **Digital Intelligence (AI/LLMs)** vs. **Hard Physical Assets (Energy, Space, Biology)**, specifically avoiding "AI-hybrid" traditional industries.
