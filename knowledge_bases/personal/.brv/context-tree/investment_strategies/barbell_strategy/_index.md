# Barbell Strategy: Structural Overview

The Barbell Strategy, originated by Nassim Taleb (*Antifragile*), is a risk management framework defined by betting on extremes while strictly avoiding the "middle ground."

### Core Architecture & Allocation
- **Conservative Pillar (90%):** Allocation to hyper-conservative, safe assets (e.g., bonds, cash) to ensure limited downside and capital preservation.
- **Aggressive Pillar (10%):** Allocation to hyper-aggressive, high-risk/high-reward assets (e.g., startups, derivatives) to capture unlimited upside.
- **Key Relationship:** This bimodal distribution promotes **antifragility** by combining extreme security with high-convexity speculation.

### Implementation Rules
- **Rule 1:** Zero allocation to medium-risk "middle" positions.
- **Rule 2:** Maintain the 90/10 ratio to balance safety and alpha.

### Modern Application: The AI Era
- **Extremes:** One end focuses on digital intelligence (LLMs/AI); the other focuses on hard physical assets (Energy, Space, Biology).
- **Strategy:** Avoid "AI + traditional industry" hybrids, which represent the inefficient middle.
