# Domain Analysis: Data Centers

The data center infrastructure landscape is undergoing a fundamental shift from semiconductor supply constraints to physical infrastructure bottlenecks, primarily driven by AI and cloud expansion. Growth is increasingly dictated by power availability, grid access, and fiber supply rather than hardware availability.

## Architectural Shifts & Infrastructure Trends
*   **Cooling Evolution**: Transitioning from traditional air cooling to **direct-to-chip liquid cooling** as a core structural requirement for AI-driven power densities.
*   **Power Architecture**: Emergence of **800V DC** and **Solid State Transformers (SST)** as long-term drivers for ultra-large campuses (100-200MW+) and extreme rack densities.
*   **Deployment Models**: Increasing favor for **modular solutions** and end-to-end providers (e.g., Eaton, Schneider Electric) to improve speed-to-market. Schneider is notably increasing rack capacity by ~20%.
*   **Supply Chain Constraints**: A significant fiber supply-demand imbalance is expected to persist for 2-3 years. In China, fiber prices have already spiked from $2/km to $9/km.

## Key Market Dynamics
*   **Market Inflection**: The European data center market is projected to reach a major inflection point between 2027 and 2029.
*   **Procurement Rules**: Delivery capability and technical specification currently supersede price in market importance.
*   **Strategic Entities**: Key industrial players shaping the infrastructure include ABB, Carrier, Eaton, Fujikura, Schneider Electric, and Siemens Energy.
