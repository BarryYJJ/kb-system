# Domain Summary: Market Trends (Level d2)

The global market landscape in early 2026 is characterized by a bifurcated focus on the rapid expansion of the Chinese AI ecosystem and the high-stakes geopolitical sensitivities of the energy sector. These domains represent the primary drivers of current structural shifts in technology and global economics.

## I. Chinese AI Ecosystem (ai_china)
The Chinese AI sector is defined by a strategic split between consumer-facing giants and enterprise-focused labs, supported by a dominant position in the global LLM token export market.

### Strategic Market Segmentation
*   **Consumer Giants:** **ByteDance (Doubao)**, **Alibaba (Qwen/DingTalk)**, and **Baidu (Ernie)** are leveraging massive domestic data and existing app ecosystems to dominate vertical industries like Finance and Healthcare.
*   **Enterprise Labs:** **MiniMax**, **Zhipu AI**, **Moonshot**, and **DeepSeek** are prioritizing autonomous agents, coding capabilities, and local government (2B/2G) deployments.
*   *Drill-down: [ai_china/ubs_china_ai_expert_call_key_takeaways.md]*

### Global Token Export Strategy
China has captured **39% of the global inference market** (up from 2%) through aggressive cost-leadership.
*   **Infrastructure:** Utilization of domestic **RTX 5090** clusters and low-cost electricity allows for API pricing at **1/6th** of the global average.
*   **Operational Model:** Compute remains domestic while marketing/front-end operations are handled overseas. Data exports are restricted primarily to non-sensitive categories like coding.
*   *Drill-down: [ai_china/china_llm_token_export_analysis.md]*

### Technological Pillars
*   **Multimodal & Agents:** High investment in video generation for e-commerce and high-context productivity tools.
*   **Efficiency:** Focus on domestic infrastructure optimization despite average export latencies of ~100 seconds.
*   *Drill-down: [ai_china/context.md]*

## II. Energy and Geopolitics (energy)
The energy domain focuses on the intersection of oil supply chains and geopolitical stability, specifically regarding US-Iran relations and global inflation.

### Strategic Leverage and Economic Thresholds
*   **Conflict Mechanics:** Energy is analyzed as a strategic bargaining chip where supply threats lead to price surges, directly impacting US domestic policy through inflation.
*   **Critical Geography:** The **Strait of Hormuz** (20% of global oil supply) remains the primary global leverage point.
*   **Economic Red Lines:** 
    - **Price Ceiling:** US political tolerance erodes at **$90–$100/barrel**. 
    - **CPI Correlation:** Every $10 increase in oil price correlates to a **~0.2% rise in US CPI**.
*   **Policy Rule:** When oil prices exceed $90 (pushing inflation >3%), US strategic gains are typically deprioritized in favor of domestic economic resolution.
*   *Drill-down: [energy/oil_price_impact_on_us_iran_conflict.md]*

### Domain Scope
*   **Included:** Oil/gas price analysis, geopolitical supply impacts, and energy-related economic indicators.
*   **Excluded:** Renewable energy technology and domestic utility regulations (unless directly impacting oil/gas markets).
*   *Drill-down: [energy/context.md]*

## III. Key Relationships & Dependencies
*   **Infrastructure Dependency:** The Chinese AI export surge relies heavily on domestic cloud providers (**Volcengine**, **Alibaba Cloud**) and Southeast Asian subsea cable expansions.
*   **Political Sensitivity:** Both domains are highly sensitive to domestic political cycles—US conflict tolerance is tied to election-year inflation, while Chinese AI labs are pivoting toward local 2B/2G deployments to ensure domestic stability.
*   **Cross-Domain Impact:** Energy price fluctuations serve as a macro-economic backdrop that influences the investment capacity for high-compute AI infrastructure globally.
